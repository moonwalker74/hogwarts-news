Houses, An Obsolete System?

Founded in 990 A.D., Hogwarts always had Houses representing values cherished by the four founders: Godric Gryffindor, Helga Hufflepuff, Rowena Ravenclaw and Salazar Slytherin. They all wanted to bring out certain qualities in the students belonging in their respective houses. Students eat, sleep, study along their housemates. The whole House system discourage them from fraternizing between Houses, it brings up competition. What if we got rid of it all?

Muggles:
Should We Care?

With the push for the new Mandatory Muggle Studies Act, we should ask ourselves important questions about our relationship to Muggles and their inventions. While some push to challenge the International Statute of Wizarding Secrecy, others just want to eradicate anything referring to Muggles in our world.

Non-Wizards

Don't Miss Out! ☚

It has been 15 years since the Remus Lupin Act passed and the controversial but much-needed change to the Ministry Classification of Magical Creatures has been put in place, where do we stand on our treatment of Magical Creatures. Goblins, Vampires, Werewolves, House-elves, Centaurs, all demand specific rights but can hundreds of years of exploitation and ill-treatment be cancelled by a few reforms?
